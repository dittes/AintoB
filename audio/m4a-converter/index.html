What is M4A?

M4A is Apple's container format for AAC-encoded audio. It delivers excellent audio quality at low bitrates — significantly better than MP3 at equivalent sizes — and is the default format for Apple Music, iTunes purchases, and voice recordings on Apple devices.

Common uses for M4A

  • Music from Apple Music or iTunes
  • High-quality audio on Apple devices (iPhone, iPad, Mac)
  • Podcast audio and voice recordings within the Apple ecosystem
Note: Some older devices and media players may not support .m4a files and require conversion to MP3.

Compatibility: Native on Apple devices, iTunes, QuickTime, and most modern media players including VLC and Windows Media Player.
