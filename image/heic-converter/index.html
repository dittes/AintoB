What is HEIC?

HEIC is the photo format used by Apple devices since iOS 11. Based on the HEIF standard with H.265 compression, HEIC files are roughly half the size of equivalent JPGs while maintaining superior image quality. They also support depth maps, transparency, and multi-image sequences for Live Photos.

Common uses for HEIC

  • Sharing photos taken on iPhone or iPad
  • Reducing iCloud storage with smaller file sizes
  • Archiving high-quality iPhone photography for editing
Note: Limited native support outside Apple ecosystems — Windows, Android, and many apps require conversion or a codec plugin.

Compatibility: Native on iOS 11+, macOS High Sierra+. Requires plugins or conversion for Windows and Android.
